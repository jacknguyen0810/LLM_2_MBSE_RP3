- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles 
- Automate observations based on a pre-determined schedule and allow uplinking of priority scenarios 
- Perform primary target observations for a lifetime of 1 month, with a goal of 12 months
- Perform observations with a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees 
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for scientific observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees 
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m 
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Maintain a temporal resolution of at least 16 days for primary target observations, with a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target, ideally before target capture begins
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days 
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 image sets 
- Transfer data between subsystems for downlink or additional storage 
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include operation modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes 
- Qualify mission hardware to levels specified in GSFC-STD-7000B
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream images from cameras as per specified requirements
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude accurately during image capture
- Monitor health of payload components including cameras and computer
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry as required
- Accommodate different imaging modes for visual spectrum and IR cameras
- Operate within specified temperature range and environmental conditions
- Implement structural requirements for robustness and alignment maintenance
- Ensure thermal ratings of all payload components meet mission requirements.
