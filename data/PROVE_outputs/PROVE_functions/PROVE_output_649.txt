- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window in MIS-001 and MIS-003 to provide a range of viewing angles
- Perform automated observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize uplink observation scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) and a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) and a goal of 15m
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images, with a goal of 50, per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Ensure a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days, with a goal of 5 days
- Maintain a minimum NedT of the IR camera of 0.5K with a goal of 0.05K
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera a minimum of once per target, ideally before target capture
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data from an observation pass within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for a threshold of one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe modes, and other TBD non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to the levels and durations specified in GSFC-STD-7000B
