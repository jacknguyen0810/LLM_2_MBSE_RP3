**System Functions:**

- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum observation elevation angle of 30 degrees
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for scientific observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Ensure temporal resolution for primary target observations of at least 16 days
- Maintain a minimum NedT of the IR camera at 0.5K
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target within one SSD over land
- Achieve pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Support different modes of operation including imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes
- Ensure electronics subsystem provides sufficient power for all system modes
- Monitor the health of payload components, including cameras and computer
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between various components
- Interface with the FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream images from cameras per observation pass
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at the moment of image capture
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Operate in different modes and accommodate imaging sequences
- Operate within specified temperature ranges and environmental conditions
- Ensure structural integrity to support payload components and fit within specified volume
- Ensure payload components are rated to withstand expected temperature range
