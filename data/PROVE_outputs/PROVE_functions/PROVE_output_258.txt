- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain imager field of views alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m (threshold) and 90m (goal) at nadir
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD of 50m (threshold) and 15m (goal) at nadir
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold) and 7 days (goal) of observation
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set (MIS-012) and ideally 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg, power usage to 14W, and volume to 2U
- Have operational modes including imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream at least 10 images from each camera per primary observation pass
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude accurately at image capture moment
- Monitor health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components and payload computer
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry for specified durations
- Accommodate different imaging modes and sequences for IR and VIS cameras
- Operate in a temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant of on-orbit radiation exposure and single event upsets/latch-ups
- Operate with a power consumption of less than 4W
- Support payload components in required vibration and structural test levels
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths and fit within specified volume
- Ensure payload components are rated to withstand mission temperature requirements.
