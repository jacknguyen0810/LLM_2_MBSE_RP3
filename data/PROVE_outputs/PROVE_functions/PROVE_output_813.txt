- **Observation Functionality**:
  - Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single observation pass.
  - Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single observation pass.
  - Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single observation pass.
  - Distribute images over the observation window for varying viewing angles.
  - Perform automated observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios.
  - Ensure a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations.

- **Imaging Specifications**:
  - Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations.
  - Maintain alignment of imager field of views to within 12 degrees.
  - Achieve spatial sampling distances at nadir for IR (300m threshold, 90m goal) and VIS (50m threshold, 15m goal) images.
  - Provide a minimum number of science images per observation pass.

- **Mission Timing and Data Handling**:
  - Perform primary target observations for a specified lifetime with defined temporal resolutions.
  - Downlink image data within specified timeframes.
  - Ensure onboard storage capacity for image sets and transfer data to the bus for downlink or storage.

- **Payload and System Limitations**:
  - Maintain payload mass, power, and volume within specified limits.
  - Include necessary modes for operational flexibility and system safety.
  - Ensure hardware qualification to specified standards.

- **Electrical System Functions**:
  - Provide sufficient power for all system modes and components.
  - Monitor health of payload components and cut power when necessary.
  - Manage electrical connections for data interfaces and grounding.

- **Software System Functions**:
  - Interface with specific cameras for command and data handling.
  - Monitor health of payload components and switch power accordingly.
  - Manage imaging modes, storage, and operational sequences.

- **Structural and Thermal Functions**:
  - Support payload components structurally and ensure alignment of imager swaths.
