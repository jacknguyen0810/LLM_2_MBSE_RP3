- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass.
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass.
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass.
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles for images in MIS-001 and MIS-003.
- Perform observations automatically based on a pre-determined schedule, with the ability to prioritize observation scenarios.
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for observations.
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for scientific observations.
- Achieve a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images.
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees.
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m at nadir, with a goal of 90m.
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD of 50m at nadir, with a goal of 15m.
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass, with a goal of 50.
- Capture a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass.
- Provide temporal resolution of 16 days for primary target observations, with a goal of 5 days.
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target and ideally before target capture begins.
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land.
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree.
- Downlink image data within 1 month threshold, with a goal of 7 days.
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets.
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage.
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg.
- Limit total payload power usage to 14W.
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U.
- Provide operational modes including imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes.
- Qualify mission hardware to the levels and durations specified in GSFC-STD-7000B.
