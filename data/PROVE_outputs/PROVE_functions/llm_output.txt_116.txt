- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles in primary and tertiary observation passes
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain imager field of view alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m at nadir, with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD of 50m at nadir, with a goal of 15m
- Provide minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide minimum of 1 science image per secondary observation pass per target
- Ensure temporal resolution of primary target observations is at least 16 days, with a goal of 5 days
- Maintain a minimum NedT of the IR camera of 0.5K, with a goal of 0.05K
- Perform radiometric calibration of IR camera at least once per target
- Provide target geolocation accuracy within one SSD over land
- Achieve pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month threshold, with a goal of 7 days
- Ensure onboard storage capacity for one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg, power usage to 14W, and volume to 2U
- Support operational modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
