- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a predetermined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain imager field of views alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance of 300m at nadir (threshold), 90m (goal)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a spatial sampling distance of 50m at nadir (threshold), 15m (goal)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Maintain temporal resolution for primary target observations at a minimum of 16 days (threshold), 5 days (goal)
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one spatial sampling distance over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold), 7 days (goal) of observation pass
- Onboard storage sized for one primary target image set (MIS-012), with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations

For electrical requirements, the system must:
- Provide sufficient power for every system mode
- Provide power via 12V, 5V, and 3V buses for components operating on those voltages
- Monitor the health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between payload components
- Provide sufficient grounding for the payload

For software requirements, the system must:
- Interface with specific IR and VIS cameras for command and data handling
- Stream images from cameras as required during observation passes
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture
- Monitor health of payload components and switch power to cameras when necessary
- Interface with CubeSat bus and manage data interfaces
- Have sufficient memory for storing imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Accommodate different operational modes and imaging sequences
- Operate within specified temperature ranges and environmental conditions
