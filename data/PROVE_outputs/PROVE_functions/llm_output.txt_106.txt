- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window in MIS-001 and MIS-003, offering a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a predetermined schedule of standard targets, with the ability to prioritize observation scenarios
- Maintain a minimum observation elevation angle of 30 degrees for targets
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for scientific observations
- Maintain an alignment of imager field of views to within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) and 90m (goal)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) and 15m (goal)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Ensure a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days and a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration at least once per target, ideally before target capture begins
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set (MIS-012) with a goal of 10 primary target image sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Incorporate different modes of operation: imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as necessary
- Qualify mission hardware to the levels and durations specified in GSFC-STD-7000B.
