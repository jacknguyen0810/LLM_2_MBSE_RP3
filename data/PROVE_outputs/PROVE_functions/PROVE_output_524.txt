- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass

- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Capture a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution of 16 days for primary target observations, with a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month threshold, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Have operational modes including imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to specified levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for all system modes
- Monitor health of payload components including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by payload computer or autonomously
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components
- Interface with and send commands to IR and VIS cameras
- Stream images from cameras as required for observations
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture
- Monitor health of camera modules and switch power automatically if needed
- Interface with CubeSat bus and CDH subsystem
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Operate in different modes and switch between them as required
- Operate within specified temperature range and environmental conditions
- Ensure structural integrity to support components and fit within specified volume
