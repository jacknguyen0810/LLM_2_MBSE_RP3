- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations automatically based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (goal: 90m)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (goal: 15m)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass (goal: 50)
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (goal: 7 days) of observation pass
- Provide onboard storage for at least one primary target image set (MIS-012) (goal: 10 sets)
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Have imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to standards specified in GSFC-STD-7000B

These functions cover a wide range of requirements from imaging capabilities to power management and structural integrity.
