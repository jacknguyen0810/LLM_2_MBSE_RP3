- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for different viewing angles
- Perform automated observations based on a pre-determined schedule with priority uplink capability
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), goal of 15m
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Capture a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Maintain a temporal resolution of 16 days (minimum) for primary target observations, goal of 5 days
- Ensure a minimum NedT of the IR camera of 0.5K, goal of 0.05K, with uncooled IR camera
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera before target capture
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold), goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set (MIS-012), goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include operational modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
