- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for varied viewing angles in primary and tertiary observations
- Perform observations automatically based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum observation elevation angle of 30 degrees
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain an alignment of the imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Provide geolocation of the target within one SSD over land
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month
- Resize onboard storage for at least one primary target image set
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg
- Limit total payload power usage to 14W
- Limit total payload volume to 2U
- Integrate imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware components as per GSFC-STD-7000B requirements
