- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to offer a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of the imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution of 16 days for primary target observations, with a goal of 5 days
- Ensure the IR camera has a NedT of a minimum 0.5K with a goal of 0.05K
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera before target capture begins
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg
- Limit total payload power usage to 14W
- Limit total payload volume to 2U
- Include imaging, standby, and safe modes, with additional non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for all system modes via a 12V, 5V, and 3V bus
- Monitor the health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between payload components, cameras, and the bus
- Interface with the FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream images from the cameras per observation pass
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at the moment of image capture
- Monitor health of camera modules and switch power to them as needed
- Interface with the CubeSat bus and CDH subsystem
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Operate in different modes with automatic switching between modes
- Accommodate different imaging sequences for visual spectrum and IR cameras
- Operate within specified temperature ranges and environmental conditions
- Withstand random vibration, sine sweep, and sine burst tests as per GSFC-STD-7000B
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths
- Fit within a specified volume
- Ensure payload components meet thermal requirements for expected temperature ranges and operating/storage conditions.
