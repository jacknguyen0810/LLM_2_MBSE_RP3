- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) and 90m (goal)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) and 15m (goal)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution of 16 days (minimum) and 5 days (goal) for primary target observations
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold) and 7 days (goal) after observation pass
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set (MIS-012) with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg, power usage to 14W, and volume to 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for all system modes via 12V, 5V, and 3V buses
- Monitor health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream images from cameras per observation pass and store imaging data
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture moment
- Monitor health of camera modules and switch power automatically if necessary
- Interface with CubeSat bus and CDH subsystem
- Operate in different modes and accommodate imaging sequences for visual and IR cameras
- Operate in a temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant of on-orbit radiation exposure and protect against single event upsets (SEUs) and latch-ups
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths and fit within a specified volume
- Ensure all payload components meet operating and storage temperature requirements
