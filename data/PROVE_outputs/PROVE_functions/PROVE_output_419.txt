- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on predetermined schedule and allow uplink of priority scenarios
- Maintain minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m (threshold), 90m (goal) at nadir
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD of 50m (threshold), 15m (goal) at nadir
- Provide minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Maintain temporal resolution for primary target observations of 16 days (minimum), 5 days (goal)
- Perform radiometric calibration of IR camera at least once per target, ideally before capture
- Provide geolocation of target within one SSD over land
- Achieve pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold), 7 days (goal) of observation pass
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set (MIS-012), with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Implement imaging, standby, safe modes and other non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to specified levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for every system mode
- Monitor health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide necessary electrical connections to support data interfaces between components
- Interface CDH subsystem with IR and VIS cameras, and spacecraft bus
- Stream images from cameras per observation pass
- Determine position and attitude of spacecraft at image capture moment
- Monitor health of camera modules, switching power if operating unsafely
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Operate in different modes and accommodate imaging sequences for cameras
- Operate in temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant of on-orbit radiation exposure and protect against single event upsets
- Provide structural support for payload components in specified vibration and shock environments
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths and fit within specified volume
- Ensure all components are rated to withstand expected temperature range and have specified operating/storage temperature limits.
