- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass

- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios

- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views to within 12 degrees

- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), goal of 15m

- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data from an observation pass within 1 month (threshold), goal of 7 days
- Onboard storage sized for one primary target image set (MIS-012), goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U

- Interface with components operating on 12V, 5V, and 3V for power supply
- Monitor health of payload components, including cameras and computer

- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components
- Interface with CubeSat bus and own CDH subsystem
- Retrieve commands from the main satellite bus and send imaging and telemetry data
- Provide sufficient storage for imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Prioritize data overwrite if memory is full
- Operate in different modes with automatic switching
- Accommodate different imaging sequences for visual spectrum and IR cameras
- Operate in a temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant to on-orbit radiation exposure and single event upsets/latch-ups
- Operate with a total power consumption of less than 4W
- Support payload components within specified vibration and structural requirements
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths
- Fit the entire payload within a 200x110x100mm volume
