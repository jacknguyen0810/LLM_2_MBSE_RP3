- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain imager field of views alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) with a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days with a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera once per target, ideally before target capture
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure the total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure the total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure the total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Support different spacecraft modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes as needed
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Provide sufficient power for all system modes via 12V, 5V, and 3V buses
- Monitor health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components
- Interface with CubeSat bus and own CDH subsystem
- Have sufficient storage for imaging data and health telemetry
- Operate in different modes and switch between imaging modes as required
- Ensure operation in a temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant to satellite bus system overrides and have fail-safes in place
- Prioritize data storage and overwrite data when memory is full, oldest first
- Ensure structural integrity to withstand random vibration, sine sweep, and sine burst tests
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths
- Ensure payload components are rated for expected temperature range and have specific operating and storage temperature limits.
