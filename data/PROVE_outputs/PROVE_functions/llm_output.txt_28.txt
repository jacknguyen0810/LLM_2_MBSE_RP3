- The system shall provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- The system shall provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- The system shall provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- The system shall distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles for primary and tertiary targets
- The system shall perform primary target observations for a duration of 1 month (threshold), with a goal of 12 months
- Observations shall be automated based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- The minimum elevation angle for target observations shall be 30 degrees
- The system shall provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- The minimum swath width for scientific images shall be 80 km at nadir
- The system shall maintain an alignment of the imager field of views within 12 degrees
- The system shall provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m at nadir (threshold), with a goal of 90m
- The system shall provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD of 50m at nadir (threshold), with a goal of 15m
- The system shall provide a minimum of 10 science images (threshold) per primary or tertiary observation pass, with a goal of 50
- The system shall provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
