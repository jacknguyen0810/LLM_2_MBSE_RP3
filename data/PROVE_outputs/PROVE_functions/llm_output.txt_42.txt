- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute IR and VIS images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform primary target observations for a minimum 16 days, with a goal of 5 days
- Support automated observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain the imager field of views alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) and a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) and a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass, with a goal of 50
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Radiometrically calibrate the IR camera at least once per target, ideally before target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Onboard storage for a primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Maintain payload mass below 2.66kg, power usage below 14W, and volume below 2U
- Provide operation modes including imaging, standby, safe, and potentially other non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware per GSFC-STD-7000B standards
