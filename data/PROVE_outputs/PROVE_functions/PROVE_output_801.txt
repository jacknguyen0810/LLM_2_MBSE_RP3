- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance at nadir of 300m (threshold) and 90m (goal)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with spatial sampling distance at nadir of 50m (threshold) and 15m (goal)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation accuracy of within one spatial sampling distance over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold) and 7 days (goal)
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set (MIS-012), with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for every system mode
- Monitor health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by payload computer or autonomously
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between payload components
- Interface CDH subsystem with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream images from cameras per observation pass as specified
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture
- Monitor health of camera modules and switch power automatically if operating unsafely
- Interface CDH subsystem with CubeSat bus and other systems
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry as required
- Operate in different modes and switch between them automatically
- Operate within specified temperature range and other environmental conditions
- Meet structural requirements for payload support and alignment
- Meet thermal requirements for all payload components temperature ratings.
