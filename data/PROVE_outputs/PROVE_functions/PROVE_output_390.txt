- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Ensure a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain imager field of views alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images, with a goal of 50, per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days, with a goal of 5 days
- Ensure IR camera NedT of a minimum of 0.5K with a goal of 0.05 K
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Support system modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Monitor health of payload components, including cameras and computer
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components and payload computer
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture moment
- Store imaging data, position data, health telemetry, and commands
- Operate in different modes and switch between them as required
- Operate in temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Tolerant to on-orbit radiation exposure and single event upsets/latch-ups
- Fit within a 200X110X100mm volume
- Rated to withstand expected temperature range during the mission
