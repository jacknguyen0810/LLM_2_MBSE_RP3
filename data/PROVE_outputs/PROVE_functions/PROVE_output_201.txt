- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold) after observation pass
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Maintain operational modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera for CDH subsystem
- Monitor health of payload components, including cameras and computer
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components and payload computer
- Determine position and attitude of spacecraft at image capture
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry as specified
- Accommodate different imaging modes and sequences for IR and VIS cameras
- Operate within specified temperature range and environmental conditions
- Ensure structural integrity to support payload components and fit within specified volume
- Ensure all payload components meet thermal requirements for temperature ranges and operating/storage conditions.
