- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain an alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set, goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Meet total payload mass limit of 2.66kg
- Meet total payload power usage limit of 14W
- Meet total payload volume limit of 2U
- Have operational modes including imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as needed
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream at least 10 images from each camera per primary observation pass
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at the moment of image capture
- Provide sufficient power for all system modes and components operating on different voltage buses
- Monitor health of payload components, cut power to cameras if necessary
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components and payload computer
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Operate in temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C
- Be tolerant to radiation exposure and single event upsets/latch-ups
- Have a chance of critical failure less than 5% over 1 month mission
- Qualify hardware to specified levels and durations
