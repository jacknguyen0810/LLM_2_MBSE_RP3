- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass

- Perform observations automatically based on a predetermined schedule, with the ability to prioritize observations

- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain imager field of views alignment within 12 degrees

- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m

- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass

- Perform radiometric calibration of IR camera at least once per target, ideally before target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U

- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for components in every system mode
- Provide power via 12V, 5V, and 3V buses for respective components
- Monitor health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide electrical connections supporting data interfaces between payload components
- Interface CDH subsystem with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Capable of sending commands to cameras and streaming images as required
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude accurately at image capture moment
- Monitor health of camera modules, temperature, and power consumption
- Switch power to camera modules automatically if operating unsafely
- Interface CDH subsystem with CubeSat bus and own CDH subsystem
- Retrieve and send imaging and telemetry data to the main satellite bus
- Store imaging data, position data, health telemetry, and commands as specified
- Prioritize data overwriting if memory is full
- Operate CDH subsystem in various modes and imaging sequences
- Accommodate different imaging sequences for IR and VIS cameras
- Operate in a temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant to on-orbit radiation exposure and single event upsets/latch-ups
- Operate with a total power consumption of less than 4W
- Support payload components in random vibration frequency spectrum as per GSFC-STD-7000B
- Withstand specified test levels and durations for structural integrity
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths and fit within specified volume
- Ensure all payload components meet thermal operating and storage temperature requirements.
