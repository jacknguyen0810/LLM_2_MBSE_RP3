- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window, providing a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a predetermined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure the total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure the total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure the total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes as needed
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Stream at least 10 images from each camera per primary observation pass
- Be able to determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture moment
- Monitor health of payload components, including cameras and computer
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components and payload computer
- Provide power for components operating on 12V, 5V, and 3V buses
- Switch power to cameras triggered by payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry as specified
- Operate in different modes and imaging sequences
- Be capable of operation in a temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Be tolerant to radiation exposure and single event upsets/latch-ups
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths to specified requirements
- Ensure all payload components are rated for expected temperature range and have specified operating/storage temperature limits
- Meet structural requirements for vibration, launch, and structural support
- Provide radiometric calibration of the IR camera before target capture begins.
