- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window in MIS-001 and MIS-003 to provide a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations automatically based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for scientific observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance at nadir of 300m (threshold) and 90m (goal)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a spatial sampling distance at nadir of 50m (threshold) and 15m (goal)
- Produce a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Perform primary target observations with a minimum temporal resolution of 16 days and a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target, preferably before target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one spatial sampling distance over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data from an observation pass within 1 month (threshold) and 7 days (goal)
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure the total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure the total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure the total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Support system modes including imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as needed
- Qualify mission hardware to the levels and durations specified in GSFC-STD-7000B.
