- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with priority uplink capability
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views to within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with SSD at nadir of 300m (threshold), goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), goal of 15m
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Capture a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Perform primary target observations with a temporal resolution of 16 days (minimum), goal of 5 days
- Ensure the IR camera NedT is a minimum of 0.5K, goal of 0.05K, with radiometric calibration performed per target
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold), goal of 7 days
- Allocate onboard storage for one primary target image set (MIS-012), goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg, power usage to 14W, and volume to 2U
- Include operational modes for imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes as required
- Qualify mission hardware to levels and durations specified in GSFC-STD-7000B
