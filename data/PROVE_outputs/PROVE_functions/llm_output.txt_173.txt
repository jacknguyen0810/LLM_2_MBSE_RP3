- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations automatically based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain imager field of view alignment within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) with a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days with a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target, ideally before target capture begins
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set (MIS-012) with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations
- Provide sufficient power for every system mode
- Monitor the health of payload components, including temperature and power consumption
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously via health monitoring sensors
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between payload components and the payload computer
- Interface with the FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude accurately at the moment of image capture
- Monitor health of camera modules and switch power automatically if operating unsafely
- Interface with the CubeSat bus and CDH subsystem
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry data as specified
- Operate in different modes with automatic switching as needed
- Accommodate various imaging sequences for visual spectrum and IR cameras
- Operate within specified temperature ranges and environmental conditions
- Withstand random vibration, sine sweep, and sine burst tests as per GSFC-STD-7000B
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths and fit within specified volume
- Ensure all payload components can withstand expected temperature ranges and have appropriate operating/storage temperatures
