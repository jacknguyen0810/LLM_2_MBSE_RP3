- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m at nadir (goal of 90m)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD of 50m at nadir (goal of 15m)
- Provide minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Perform primary target observations with a temporal resolution of 16 days (goal of 5 days)
- Maintain a minimum NedT of the IR camera at 0.5K (goal of 0.05K)
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (goal of 7 days) of an observation pass
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set (goal of 10 sets)
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Support system modes: imaging, standby, safe, and other non-nominal modes as required
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Monitor health of payload components including cameras and computer
- Provide power via 12V, 5V, and 3V buses for components
- Cut power to cameras triggered by the payload computer or autonomously
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components and payload computer
- Retrieve and store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Prioritize data overwriting when memory is full
- Support operation modes including imaging sequences and safe mode
- Operate in temperature range of -30 to +50 degrees C under vacuum conditions
- Ensure system reliability with a chance of critical failure less than 5%
- Tolerate at least 7 months of on-orbit radiation exposure
