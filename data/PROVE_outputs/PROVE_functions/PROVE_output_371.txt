- Capture multiple IR and VIS images of a primary target during a single pass
- Capture IR images of secondary targets during a single pass
- Capture multiple VIS images of a tertiary target during a single pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide varying viewing angles
- Perform primary target observations for a minimum of 1 month, with a goal of 12 months
- Automate observations based on a pre-determined schedule, with ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), goal of 15m
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Capture a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution of 16 days for primary target observations, goal of 5 days
- Ensure the IR camera has a NedT of 0.5K (threshold), goal of 0.05K, and is uncooled
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation of the target within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold), goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set (MIS-012), goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg
- Limit total payload power usage to 14W
- Limit total payload volume to 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe modes, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B levels and durations.
