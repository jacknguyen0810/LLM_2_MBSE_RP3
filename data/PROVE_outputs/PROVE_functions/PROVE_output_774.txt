- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target in a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets in a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target in a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for a range of viewing angles
- Automate observations based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to uplink priority scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Ensure a minimum swath width of 80km at nadir for scientific images
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), goal of 15m
- Capture a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass, goal of 50
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days, goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target, ideally before target capture begins
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within a threshold of 1 month, goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg
- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W
- Ensure total payload volume does not exceed 2U
- Provide imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes as required
- Qualify mission hardware to levels and durations specified in GSFC-STD-7000B
