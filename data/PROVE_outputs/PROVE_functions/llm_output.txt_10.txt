- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute IR and VIS images over the observation window, providing a range of viewing angles
- Perform automated observations based on a pre-determined schedule of standard targets, with the ability to prioritize observation scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain payload structure alignment of imager field of views to within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with a spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold) with a goal of 90m
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold) with a goal of 15m
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images (goal of 50) per primary or tertiary observation pass, and a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution for primary target observations of a minimum of 16 days, with a goal of 5 days
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target, ideally before target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data from an observation pass within a threshold of 1 month, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set (MIS-012), with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Ensure total payload mass does not exceed 2.66kg, total power usage does not exceed 14W, and total volume does not exceed 2U
- Provide operation modes including imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal modes
- Qualify mission hardware to GSFC-STD-7000B standards.
