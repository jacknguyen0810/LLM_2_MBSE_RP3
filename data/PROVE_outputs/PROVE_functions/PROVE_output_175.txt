- Provide multiple IR and VIS images of a given primary target during a single primary observation pass
- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window for varied viewing angles
- Perform automated observations based on pre-determined schedule with priority uplink capability
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of the imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) at nadir of 300m (threshold), 90m (goal)
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD at nadir of 50m (threshold), 15m (goal)
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Ensure temporal resolution for primary target observations is a minimum of 16 days, with a goal of 5 days
- Achieve a NedT of the IR camera of 0.5K (threshold), 0.05K (goal), utilizing an uncooled camera
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation accuracy of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month (threshold), 7 days (goal) of an observation pass
- Store data for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg, power usage to 14W, and volume to 2U
- Ensure the system has imaging, standby, safe modes, and other non-nominal modes as required
- Qualify the mission hardware to the standards specified in GSFC-STD-7000B
