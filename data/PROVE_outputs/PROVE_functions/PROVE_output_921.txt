- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass

- Perform automated observations based on a pre-determined schedule with priority uplink capability

- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with a SSD of 50m (threshold), with a goal of 15m

- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass

- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target capture
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Downlink image data within 1 month threshold, with a goal of 7 days
- Size onboard storage for one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage

- Ensure total payload power usage does not exceed 14W

- Provide power for all system modes via 12V, 5V, and 3V buses

- Cut power to cameras as needed, by payload computer or autonomously
- Provide electrical connections for data interfaces between components
- Interface with FLIR Tau2 IR camera and Basler acA640-100gm camera
- Determine spacecraft position and attitude at image capture moment
- Monitor health of camera modules and switch power automatically if needed
- Interface with CubeSat bus and CDH subsystem
- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry
- Operate in different modes and switch between them automatically
- Accommodate imaging sequences for IR and VIS cameras

- Operate with total power consumption less than 4W

- Maintain alignment of imager swaths
