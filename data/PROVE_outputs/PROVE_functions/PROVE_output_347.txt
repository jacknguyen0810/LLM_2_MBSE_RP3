- Provide IR images of given secondary targets during a single secondary observation pass
- Provide multiple VIS images of a given tertiary target during a single tertiary observation pass
- Distribute images over the observation window to provide a range of viewing angles
- Perform observations automatically based on a pre-determined schedule with the ability to prioritize scenarios
- Maintain a minimum elevation angle of 30 degrees for target observations
- Provide narrow-angle IR and VIS images for science observations
- Maintain alignment of imager field of views within 12 degrees
- Provide IR images with spatial sampling distance (SSD) of 300m at nadir
- Provide narrow-angle VIS images with SSD of 50m at nadir
- Provide a minimum of 10 science images per primary or tertiary observation pass
- Provide a minimum of 1 science image per target per secondary observation pass
- Achieve a temporal resolution of 16 days for primary target observations
- Perform radiometric calibration of the IR camera at least once per target
- Provide geolocation of the target to within one SSD over land
- Achieve a pointing accuracy of 1 degree
- Downlink image data within 1 month
- Size onboard storage for at least one primary target image set, with a goal of 10 sets
- Transfer data to the bus for downlink or additional storage
- Limit total payload mass to 2.66kg
- Limit total payload power usage to 14W
- Limit total payload volume to 2U
- Include imaging, standby, safe, and other TBD non-nominal operational modes

- Store imaging data, position data, and health telemetry appropriately
- Operate in different modes, switch between modes automatically as required
- Operate within specified temperature range and environmental conditions
- Interface with the CubeSat bus system effectively
- Prioritize data storage and retrieval as needed
- Ensure structural support for payload components in various vibration and launch scenarios
- Maintain alignment of imager swaths to specified dimensions
- Ensure payload fits within specified volume constraints
