**Spacecraft**

- The Spacecraft shall not exceed a wet mass of 6,159 kilograms.
- Any launch vehicle performance enhancement or reduction shall be adjusted in the Observatory mass allocation.
- The Spacecraft and OTE shall aim for a combined reliability goal of 0.920.
- The Spacecraft shall utilize the Deep Space Network for communication.
- Commanding shall be available continuously for 90% of 4-Pi steradian coverage.
- The Spacecraft shall be in continuous two-way communication until the completion of Primary Mirror Phasing activities.
- Telemetry shall be provided from launch vehicle payload fairing separation until separation from the upper stage.
- The Spacecraft shall receive commands via low data rate channels during initial deployment and emergency communications.
- RF link margins for all links shall be at least +3dB in all operating and contingency modes.
- During normal operations contact, the Observatory shall downlink uncompressed recorded engineering data.
- The onboard data processing system shall utilize CCSDS File Data Protocol for downlink of stored science data and engineering telemetry.
- The high rate downlink shall have selectable rates of 7, 14, 28 Megabits per second.
- The Spacecraft shall receive commands via S-Band at a minimum rate of 250 bps.
- The Spacecraft shall transmit telemetry via S-Band at a minimum rate of 200 bps during safe mode.
- The Observatory shall transmit a minimum of 99.5% of all real-time telemetry to the Ground Segment.
- The Observatory shall transmit a minimum of 99.5% of all recorded data to the Ground Segment.
- The Electrical Power Subsystem shall provide conditioned power to the Observatory during all mission phases.
- The EPS shall distribute direct current power at 28 V +7/-6 at the interface connectors.
- Circuit protection devices shall protect primary power cable wiring harnesses.
- All primary power returns shall be via hardwires to the EPS single prime power ground point.
- Observatory mechanisms shall have functional redundancy or be designed to meet mission requirements despite failures.
- The thermal control system shall regulate temperatures within safe and operational limits for all mission phases.
- The Observatory architecture shall allow for passive cooling of ISIM-related components to their operational limits.
- The Observatory shall passively cool NIR Science Detectors to less than or equal to 37K during commissioning and until the end of the science mission lifetime.
- Propellant shall be sized for 10 years of operation after launch.
- The Spacecraft shall be responsible for the health and safety of the Observatory.
- The Observatory shall be autonomously single fault tolerant against all credible failures.
- The design shall preclude propagation of part failure effects beyond the component containing the part.
- Redundant Observatory functionality shall be cross-strapped, excluding primary power.
- The Observatory shall enter safe modes and reconfigure onboard systems in response to anomalies.
- Safe modes shall have a least-to-greatest hierarchical impact on the mission timeline.
- All safe modes shall conserve consumables.
- The Observatory shall sustain an autonomous safe mode for 4 weeks without Ground Segment intervention.
- The Observatory shall exit safe modes via Ground Segment commands.
- In Safe Haven mode, the Observatory shall retain and downlink recorded data and real-time engineering data.
- The JWST Observatory shall meet the interface requirements to the Launch Segment as defined in the Application to Use Ariane IRD.
