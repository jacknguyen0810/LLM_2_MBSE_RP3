**Spacecraft Subsystem**

- The Spacecraft shall be responsible for the health and safety of the Observatory.
- The Spacecraft shall be autonomously single fault tolerant against all credible failures that may result in loss of the mission.
- The design of the Spacecraft shall preclude propagation of the effects of part failures beyond the component containing the part that fails.
- Redundant Spacecraft functionality shall be cross-strapped.
- The Spacecraft shall enter safe modes and reconfigure its onboard systems in response to anomalies when configured for safe mode entry.
- All safe modes shall be designed to conserve consumables.
- The Spacecraft shall sustain an autonomous safe mode for 4 weeks without Ground Segment intervention.
- The Spacecraft shall exit safe modes via Ground Segment commands.
- While in Safe Haven mode the Spacecraft shall retain recorded data while simultaneously down-linking the recorded data and real-time engineering data to support anomaly investigation and recovery to normal operations.

**Communication Subsystem**

- The Spacecraft shall be designed to ensure that commanding is available on a continuous basis for 90% of 4-Pi steradian coverage.
- The Spacecraft shall be in continuous two-way communication with the Ground Segment from separation from the upper stage of the launch vehicle until the completion of Observatory Primary Mirror Phasing activities.
- The Spacecraft shall provide telemetry to the Ground Segment from launch vehicle payload fairing separation until separation from the upper stage of the launch vehicle.
- The Spacecraft shall utilize the Deep Space Network to communicate with the Ground Segment.
- The Spacecraft shall be available to receive commands via the low data rate channels during initial deployment and in events requiring emergency communications.
- Radio frequency link margins for all links shall be at least +3dB in all operating and contingency modes.
- During a normal operations contact, the Spacecraft shall downlink the uncompressed recorded engineering data.
- The onboard data processing system shall utilize the Consultative Committee on Space Data Systems protocol for downlink of stored science data and engineering telemetry.
- During a normal operations contact, the Spacecraft shall be capable of downlinking to the Ground Segment 229 Gigabits of science data, which was compressed from 458 Gigabits.
- The onboard data processing system shall utilize the CCSDS protocol for real-time downlink of engineering telemetry.
- The S-band link shall be used for ranging the Observatory.
- COP-1 and CFDP shall be utilized for command uplink.
- The command uplink shall be S-Band.
- The command uplink shall be at 250 bps for low rate, 2 Kbps for medium rate, and 16 Kbps for high rate.
- The downlink shall be Reed-Solomon encoded.
- JWST data encoding on the Observatory shall include CCSDS randomization encoding for transmission to the ground.
- The low rate downlink shall be S-Band.
- The high rate downlink shall be Ka-Band.
- The Spacecraft shall receive commands via S-Band at a minimum rate of 250 bps.
- The Spacecraft shall transmit telemetry via S-Band at a minimum rate of 200 bps during safe mode.
- The Observatory shall transmit a minimum of 99.5% of all real-time telemetry to the Ground Segment.
- The Observatory shall transmit a minimum of 99.5% of all recorded data to the Ground Segment.

**Electrical Power Subsystem**

- The Electrical Power Subsystem shall provide conditioned power to the Observatory during all mission phases.
- The EPS shall distribute direct current power to the loads at 28 V +7/-6 at the interface connectors.
- Circuit protection devices shall be sized to protect primary power cable wiring harnesses.
- All primary power returns shall be via hardwires to the EPS single prime power ground point.

**Structural and Mechanical Subsystem**

- Observatory mechanisms shall have functional redundancy or be designed, manufactured, integrated, and tested to high reliability standards.

**Thermal Subsystem**

- The thermal control system shall regulate the temperatures and rates of temperature changes of the JWST Observatory within safe and operational limits.
- The Observatory architecture shall allow for the passive cooling of ISIM-related components and electronics to their safe and operational limits.
- The Observatory shall passively cool the Near-Infrared Science Detectors to a temperature of less than or equal to 37K.

**Propellant Lifetime**

- Propellant shall be sized for 10 years of operation after launch.

**Launch Vehicle Interface**

- The JWST Observatory shall meet the interface requirements to the Launch Segment defined in the Application to Use Ariane IRD.
